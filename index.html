It's hot. Phoenix is the most populous city of the Phoenix metropolitan area, also known as the Valley of the Sun, which in turn is part of the Salt River Valley and Arizona Sun Corridor. The metro area is the 10th-largest by population in the United States with approximately 4.85 million people as of 2020, making it the most populous in the Southwestern United States. Phoenix, the seat of Maricopa County, is the largest city by area in Arizona, with an area of 517.9 square miles (1,341 km2), and is also the 11th-largest city by area in the United States.

Phoenix Zoo: Located in Papago Park, the Phoenix Zoo sprawls across 125 acres and houses a diverse array of animals. From Asian elephants to Komodo dragons, baboons, giraffes, and Sumatran tigers, this zoo promises an exciting adventure for animal lovers and families. Don't miss the Red Barn petting zoo and the narrated safari tour! Some attractions will require additional prices.

Arizona Science Center: Right in downtown Phoenix, the Arizona Science Center offers hands-on science exhibits across four floors. Explore topics like friction, gravity, the solar system, biology, and more. The planetarium is a must-visit, and they even have a program called “Science With a Twist” for adults over 21.

Castles N’ Coasters: This amusement park combines golf, rides, and arcade games. Experience Desert Storm, Splashdown, and Sky Diver. Plus, they have a whopping 10,000 square feet of arcade space with classic pinball and modern games.

OdySea Aquarium: Dive into the underwater wonders at OdySea Aquarium! It’s the largest aquarium in the Southwest, featuring sharks, otters, sea lions, penguins, and more. Explore SeaTREK® underwater ocean walking, touch exhibits, and the world’s only rotating aquarium
